The rise of technology in the Bay area

Created For: CMU design alumni | Carnegie Mellon University

Our team of 5 communication design students were tasked with creating a survey targeting the design alumni of Carnegie Mellon University to gather data about their current practises and attitudes towards design. We knew we would have answers from a wide age range of alumni so we decided to focus on how our design practises have changed over the years with the influence of technology. Our group was assigned the alumni residing in the Bay area in California which was a perfect opportunity to study the way technology has influenced our design habits through the rise of Silicon Valley and the dotcom boom.

To display this data effectively we created an interactive website that allowed users to browse through this data. We also created an array of artworks and stickers in a pixel art style to accompany the responses we recieved from the alumni.


The different screens

Alongside the theme of pixel art we wanted to create the website in an old school "game-esque" kind of style that would compliment our artwork and seem more like a storytelling site. A few features such as the 8-bit music, the "hacker-green" highlight colour and including hoverable images containing information gave the website more of a techy game feel.
